Greg Monroe’s professional career began when the Detroit Pistons picked him with the seventh overall choice in the first round of the 2010 NBA draught. On July 6, 2010, he joined the club. He made his NBA season debut against the Chicago Bulls on October 30, 2010, scoring 2 points and grabbing 3 rebounds in 7 minutes off the bench.

On December 10, 2010, he earned his first career start against the Minnesota Timberwolves, with eight points, fifteen rebounds, and a block in 35 minutes of action. On February 23, 2011, he had his finest game of the season, scoring 27 points and 12 rebounds on 11–17 shooting against the Indiana Pacers.

In the 2011-2012 season, he appeared in and started all 66 games for Detroit. On February 8, 2012, he was picked to participate in the NBA All-Star Weekend Rising Stars Challenge. In the 2013-2014 season, he had his third consecutive season of 1,000 points and 600 rebounds.

Monroe accepted a qualifying offer with the Pistons on September 8, 2014, making him an unrestricted free agent in 2015. In a 102–109 overtime defeat against the Boston Celtics on December 3, 2014, he set a season-best with 29 points. After missing 11 games due to a knee injury, he returned to play on April 8, 2015.

He scored 19 points and grabbed 10 rebounds in a game against the Celtics, but the team lost. On February 8, 2018, he signed with the Boston Celtics. In a 111–104 win over the Chicago Bulls on April 6, 2018, he achieved his second career triple-double with 19 points, 11 rebounds, and 10 assists off the bench.

On August 10th, 2018, he signed with the Toronto Raptors. On February 7th, 2019, he was dealt with the Brooklyn Nets, who released him immediately. On March 24, 2019, he signed a 10-day deal with the Boston Celtics. When his 10-day deal with the Celtics expired, he was not re-signed. After that, he spent 2019 with the 76ers but was not re-signed.

On July 25, 2019, he joined Bayern Munich of the German Basketball Bundesliga (BBL) and EuroLeague. On July 30, 2020, he joined with Russian team Khimki of the VTB United League and the EuroLeague. Monroe’s contract with Khimki was terminated on January 23, 2021.

On November 5, 2021, he joined the NBA G League’s Capital City Go-Go. On December 27, 2021, he signed a 10-day contract with the Minnesota Timberwolves, where he made his debut against the Boston Celtics, scoring 11 points, grabbing 9 rebounds, and dishing out 6 assists in the Wolves’ 108-103 victory.

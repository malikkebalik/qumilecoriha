John Deere 8650 Price, Reviews, Specs, Engine Oil Capacity, Weight, Serial Numbers,  Attachments, History, Features & Pictures

The John Deere 8650 was the middle model of John Deere’s 50-series four-wheel drive (4WD) tractors that were first available in 1982.

Efficiency was the promotion focus, noting that these tractors could be used during the season, not simply for tillage.

The John Deere 8650 four-wheel drive tractor was able to plant and cultivate 30-inch rows, and perform a variety of farming operations with its draft-control hitch and full-power PTO.

John Deere 8650 Four-wheel Drive Tractor was equipped with a John Deere inter-cooled turbocharged diesel 6-cylinder liquid-cooled 619 ci [10.1 L] engine, which delivers 290 hp [216.3 kW] power at rated rpm 2100.

John Deere, 8650 Four-wheel Drive Tractor had a John Deere 10.1L 6-cyl diesel engine, 4×4 articulated 4WD, hydrostatic power steering, hydraulic wet disc brakes, 9,260 lbs [4200 kg] Rear lift, 29320 to 30270 pounds weight, 16 forward and 6 reverse gears and 150 qts [142.0 L] oil capacity.

Kristin Davis Plastic Surgery Star Opens up About Fillers and Online Criticism

August 2024 · 3 minute read

Kristin Davis is “an adept” of natural beauty which was evident in all of “S*x and the City” including the feature-length films. But the actress had a remarkably different look in “And Just Like That”.

When the first time she appeared in “And Just Like That” the crowd was unpleasantly shocked. Despite not being older, her face underwent a significant transformation. After the publication of the series, the audience’s responses astonished Kristin Davis.

According to Kristin Davis, she has been subjected to a flood of unfavorable remarks about her appearance on social media that have brought her to tears. The 58-year-old “And Just Like That” actor claims that online trolls have “ridiculed relentlessly” her for being frank and honest about her usage of fillers.

The star claimed that she initially dabbled with cosmetic surgery by testing out Bótox, but that it “didn’t do anything else for a long time.” Davis began experimenting with fillers at that point. She said:

“I have done fillers and it’s been good and I’ve done fillers and it’s been bad, I’ve had to get them dissolved and I’ve been ridiculed relentlessly. And I have shed tears about it. It’s very stressful.”

Why Did Kristin Davis Say We Need to Stop Judging Cosmetic Surgery?

The “And Just Like That” actress called out ladies who “shame” other people for obtaining cosmetic surgery. She has frequently found herself on the receiving end of rude comments regarding her appearance on her social media account.

The actor explained why some people want to conceal their cosmetic changes:

“I think part of the reason that people are not more open is because there is such shaming put on you, largely by other women, It’s so sad. We have enough problems as it is. We don’t need to shame each other.”

Kristin continued by claiming that criticizing others for choosing to have plastic surgery is a “waste of time” but she did agree that “it does hurt.” She continued by asking why certain people feel the need to make remarks on women’s appearances in particular.

“We’re doing the best we can do. Everybody is doing the best they can do and everyone can make their own choices, right? But you don’t criticise everybody else for their choices, or their mistakes or whatever it is. That’s not helping you. It’s not helping them. It’s not helping anybody. It’s just getting clicks or whatever.”

Earlier, while discussing the cosmetic procedures she’s had done herself, Kristin claimed that pressure from society and being on TV, where she’s constantly faced with her changing image, have both contributed to her decision. The AJLT star said:

“It’s hard to be confronted with your younger self at all times. And it’s a challenge to remember that you don’t have to look like that, The internet wants you to – but they also don’t want you to. They’re very conflicted.”
